# Mouthpiece Construction Guide

### Step 1. Eat your way to the kinder egg capsule. You can do what you want with the toy.
![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_1.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 2. Separate the top and bottom of the egg with a saw or cutting pliers
![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_2.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 3. Carefully drill 4mm holes in both through top of both capsule halves.  
> [!NOTE]  
> Ensure they are aligned fitting one upon the other before drilling. The hose should only just be able to fit through, tightly clamped by the hole to ensure a hermetic seal.

![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_3.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 4. Cut a 5cm by 5cm square of copper foil with scissors.
![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_4.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 5. Shape the foil into a circle with scissors and cut a roughly 5mm hole through the centre.
![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_5.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 6. Stick the foil to the taller capsule half as shown.  
This is the base of your mouthpiece

![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_6.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 7. Strip the top of a jumper wire to expose the wire and solder to the bottom of the copper foil as shown.

### Step 8. Cut a 5-10cm length of hose and funnel through the mouthpiece.
![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_7.JPEG "Kinder egg")

### Step 9. Funnel hose through the other capsule half (the mouthpiece top) to assemble your finished mouthpiece.
> [!NOTE]  
> Make sure that the top and base are touching with minimal air gap between them.

![alt text](https://github.com/RyanMcB8/Tromboneless/blob/feature/README/Documentation/Hardware/images/mouthp_step_8.JPEG "Kinder egg")


### (Optional) Step 10. We also attached a molex shell to the other end of the wire to ensure mechanical stability. The sensor is easily triggered by mechanical stresses upon the wire.

### Step 11. Trombone more with tromboneless!!
